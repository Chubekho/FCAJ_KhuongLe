> These are my honest reflections after 12 weeks in the **First Cloud AI Journey (FCAJ)** program, offered in the hope of contributing, even in a small way, to making the program even better for future cohorts.

### Overall Evaluation

#### 1. Learning Environment

Honestly, before joining FCAJ, I felt a bit anxious, since this was my first time engaging with a program this hands-on, a world away from the usual "learn theory, do a small assignment" style at school. But the deeper I went into the program, the friendlier and more open the environment turned out to be than I expected. Even without daily direct interaction with the FCAJ team in the way a traditional company would have, the entire set of documentation, roadmap, and community was built in a remarkably structured way, letting me self-study with clear direction instead of getting lost in the vast ocean of AWS knowledge. My own study space at home became a lot more disciplined as a result, since I was following a clear 12-week roadmap. If I could offer one suggestion, I think it would be wonderful to have periodic offline or online meetups among learners in the same cohort, so people could discuss the difficulties they're facing directly, instead of just puzzling through everything alone.

#### 2. Support from FCAJ's Documentation and Community

What impressed me most was the incredibly detailed and well-structured documentation FCAJ built — from the most basic steps like registering an AWS account, all the way to advanced labs on Serverless, Containers, and security. Whenever I got stuck on an idea or didn't understand a concept, I could go back to the documentation to cross-check, and most of the time I'd find the answer without needing to ask anyone. The AWS Study Group community was also a place I learned a great deal from, through the offline events I attended — sitting alongside other people wrestling with similar problems made this self-study journey feel a lot less lonely.

#### 3. Relevance of the Program to My Field of Study

The program's content stayed closely aligned with what I'd studied at university about computer networks, databases, and web development, while also opening up an entirely new perspective on how systems operate at a real-world scale — something a university curriculum could hardly convey in full. Having to build an actual production system with my own hands, from networking and databases to CI/CD, taught me that classroom knowledge is only a starting point, and the gap to a truly operational system is much wider than I'd ever imagined.

#### 4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities

This is probably the area where I gained the most. Beyond pure AWS technical knowledge, I also learned how to plan my own work on a weekly basis, how to write coherent technical documentation (a skill I used to think I was quite weak at), and how to debug issues on my own with no one holding my hand — something a classroom setting rarely demands. Having to research, experiment, fail, and then learn from it week after week built a far more proactive mindset in me than I had before joining the program.

#### 5. Culture & Community Spirit

The "by the community, for the community" spirit came through most clearly at the FCAJ Community Day events I attended — where the people sharing knowledge weren't distant experts, but people from within the community itself, people who had been in a learner's seat much like mine not long before. That made the journey of learning AWS feel less like something I was doing alone, and more like being part of a larger community progressing together.

#### 6. Program Policies

The program grants learners complete autonomy over their own schedule, as long as they keep pace with the 12-week roadmap — something I really appreciated, since it let me balance schoolwork and the internship. The documentation, roadmap, and community workshops were all free and accessible, which is genuinely valuable for students who don't have a lot of resources to spare.

### Additional Questions

#### What did you find most satisfying during your internship?

Without a doubt, the moment I first saw my own workshop project running live on a real domain, with the full flow working end to end — from registration, to creating a Brand Persona, to generating content with AI. That feeling was completely different from just finishing a small lab exercise; it was the first time I truly felt I could build a complete product from scratch with my own hands.

#### What do you think the program should improve for future interns?

I think adding a direct Q&A channel (even just a chat group) dedicated to each cohort, where learners could quickly ask questions when stuck on a specific step, would save a lot of time compared to figuring everything out alone — especially for people who are completely new to AWS, the way I was at the start.

#### If recommending to a friend, would you suggest they intern here? Why or why not?

Yes, I would absolutely recommend it. It's one of the few programs that lets students build and deploy a real system on AWS with their own hands while still in school, rather than just reading documentation or doing simulated exercises. The deadline pressure is real, but in return you get a level of hands-on knowledge that's hard to find anywhere else, at almost no cost.

---

### Suggestions & Expectations

#### Suggestions to improve the internship experience:

I think the program could consider breaking the final two-week phase (the workshop project implementation stage) into intermediate progress checkpoints, rather than leaving learners to manage everything entirely on their own until right before the deadline — this would help reduce the last-minute crunch I personally experienced.

#### Would you like to continue this program in the future?

Yes, I'd very much like to keep exploring the AWS services that the past 12 weeks didn't leave enough time to fully cover, especially the Data & Analytics and AI/ML areas that the program touched on but didn't go deep into.

#### **Closing Remarks:**

I'd like to sincerely thank the FCAJ team for building such a well-structured, free program, giving students like me the chance to engage with cloud technology in the most hands-on way possible. This has certainly been one of the most memorable learning experiences of my time at university.
